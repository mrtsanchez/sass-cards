Harvest Festival

The Fiesta San Mateo goes back to the 12th century, and the Wine Harvest Festival was added in 1956. During this week of revelry in Logroño, there is a carnival procession, gastronomic tastings organised by the social clubs known as “peñas”, a programme of bullfights, the San Mateo “pelota” tournament in the Adarraga “Fronton” court, concerts, theatre, live music in the street, firework displays over the river Ebro, etc.

Stilt Dance

Back in the 17th century people said that this festival had been held since time immemorial. The festival lasts about a week. The famous stilt dance is performed in which eight young men in traditional dress do somersaults off 45 cm stilts on a paved hill called Cuesta de los Danzadores (Dancers’ Hill).

Calle Laurel

The Laurel Street is famous for being a typical tapas street of the city. In just over 200 meters up this road and its environs are set more than 40 bars and restaurants. The custom is that in each bar are offered one or two specialties, ranging from the typical such as the mushroom, the ear pork or patatas bravas, to others with their own name as the "fucking great", or "Zorropito", always accompanied by wine from the region.
